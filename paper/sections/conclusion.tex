\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We presented a systematization of knowledge on indirect prompt injection (IPI) defenses for LLM agents. Our unified adaptive attack framework, applied to 13 defenses across 5 classes, reveals that every defense relying on a single observable signal collapses under adaptive attack (ASR rises to 1.00), while runtime-checking defenses resist only by over-blocking benign actions (FPR up to 1.00). Failure modes cluster cleanly into four root-cause families, each dominant for a specific defense class, validating the predictive power of our taxonomy. A single-signal impossibility theorem formalizes why: any defense whose decision is a function of one observable signal can be broken by adaptive search. Design-principle prototypes confirm that orthogonal multi-signal defenses are necessary for robustness but incur a utility cost, defining the open problem of achieving both low R-ASR and low FPR. We release our framework, benchmark, and 3{,}900 trial results as artifacts to support reproducible evaluation of future IPI defenses.
